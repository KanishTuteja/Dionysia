Privacy Policy

Effective 14 July 2026

We collect as little as we can and we don't sell your data. This policy explains what we hold, why, who helps us run the Service, and the rights you have over your information.

1Who is responsible for your data

Dionysia is operated by Kanish Tuteja, a sole proprietor based in Quebec, Canada, and is the person responsible for the personal information collected through dionysia.ca. For any privacy question or request, contact privacy@dionysia.ca.

2What we collect

  • Account information — your email address and password (stored securely; we never see it in plain text).
  • Membership & payment information — your subscription status and billing history. Card payments go directly to Stripe; we receive confirmation and limited details (such as the last four digits and card brand) but never your full card number.
  • Usage information — basic, privacy-friendly analytics about how the site is used (pages viewed, general region, device type). We use Plausible, which does not use cookies and does not track you across other sites.
  • Communications — messages you send us and our replies.
  • Creator information — if you are a featured creator, the details, titles, stills, and credits you provide for your work.

We do not knowingly collect information from children under 16.

3Why we use it

We use your information only to: create and secure your account; provide the Service and stream Content to members; process payments and manage your membership; respond to you; understand and improve the Service; and meet our legal obligations. We rely on your consent and on the need to perform our agreement with you as the basis for this use. You can withdraw consent at any time (see your rights below), though some uses are needed to keep your account working.

4Who we share it with

We don't sell your personal information and we don't share it for advertising. We use a small set of trusted service providers to run Dionysia, and share only what each needs to do its job:

We may also disclose information if the law requires it, or to protect the rights and safety of the Service, its creators, or its members.

5Cookies & tracking

We keep this simple. We use essential cookies and similar storage needed to keep you signed in and to remember basic preferences. Our analytics (Plausible) do not use tracking cookies and do not build a profile of you. We do not run advertising trackers.

6How long we keep it

We keep your account and membership information for as long as your account is active, and for a reasonable period afterwards to meet legal, tax, and accounting requirements. When information is no longer needed, we delete or anonymise it. You can ask us to close your account and delete your data at any time (see below).

7How we protect it

Passwords are encrypted, payments run over secure connections handled by Stripe, and members-only films are delivered through short-lived, signed playback tokens so they can't be streamed without an active membership. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable measures to safeguard your information and will notify you and the relevant authorities of a serious breach as the law requires.

8Your rights

Under Quebec's Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector (as modernised by Law 25) and Canada's PIPEDA, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Correct information that is inaccurate or incomplete;
  • Withdraw consent to our use of your information;
  • Delete your account and information, subject to any records we must keep by law;
  • Portability — receive the computerised information you gave us in a structured, commonly used format;
  • Complain to us and, if unsatisfied, to the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec.

To exercise any of these, email privacy@dionysia.ca. We'll respond within the time the law allows (generally 30 days) and may need to verify your identity first. The person responsible for privacy matters at Dionysia is the Operator, reachable at that address.

9Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we'll post the new version here and change the effective date above. If the change is significant, we'll let you know by email or a notice on the site.
